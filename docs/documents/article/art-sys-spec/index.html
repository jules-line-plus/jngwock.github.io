    1. Operating System Requirements

    Windows macOS iOS Android Web
    Windows 7 macOS 10.11 iOS 11.0 Android 5.0
    (min SDK 21)    		 WebPlanetKit Supported Browser

    Android requires Manifest.permission.READ_PHONE_STATE

    PlanetKit assumes cellular phone call has more priority than LINE Planet call - both 1:1 call and group call. PlanetKit will end the LINE Planet call when occurs a cellular phone call in the middle of the LINE Planet call. So the android application requires the Manifest.permission.READ_PHONE_STATE permission.

    If the application Do Not have the Manifest.permission.READ_PHONE_STATE permission, PlanetKit cannot detect that a cellular phone call occurs or not.

    4. Network Requirements

    Network characteristics are not fixed and LINE Planet controls audio and video quality to adapt the network within a call by reasoning network condition.
    For example, If there are packet lost, LINE Planet protects media packets by generating FEC packets to keep call quality.
    Also if there are speed limitation, LINE Planet lowers the video bitrate to avoid the network congestion.

    Following table shows the uni-directional required network speed(kbps) under not lossy networks.

    Please note that this is the minimum requirement for a LINE Planet call. LINE Planet drives better call quality in the higher speed network.

    1:1 Call

    Terminal Network Audio Call Video Call
    2G 13 63
    3G 20 120
    4G 20 120
    5G 26 126
    WIFI 26 126
    Ethernet 26 426

    We recommend that try
    1:1 video call in the 1.5Mbps/1.5Mbps (up/down) network.

    Group Call

    Terminal Network Audio Conference (Mixing) Video Conference(SVC)
    (For one video stream)
    2G 13 113
    3G 20 170
    4G 20 170
    5G 26 176
    WIFI 26 176
    Ethernet 26 476

    The proper speed depends on how many videos are streamed on one client.
    For example, we recommend that try
    group call in the 2Mbps/2Mbps(Up/Down) network in 4 participants joined video conference.

    Presentation

    Presentation(Screen share) video is usually HD video resolution.
    This requires 1.6Mbps/1.6Mbps (Up/Down) network.
    When a presentation is started, the presentation video has higher priority than the camera video. So the camera video resolution could be resized to the smaller resolution.

    Video resolution and bandwidth

    The video resolution is controlled by the network speed.
    The uni-directional minimum network speed(kbps) for each resolution under not lossy networks are following.

    Video Call Resolution 1:1 Video Call Video Conference(SVC)
    QVGA 50 100
    VGA 100 250
    HD 1000 1150
